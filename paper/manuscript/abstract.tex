\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Population genomics tools operate on flat files with matrix-based computation, producing ephemeral results disconnected from functional annotations. This architecture precludes analyses that require conditioning statistics on annotation categories or integrating signals across independently computed measures. Here we present GraphPop, a graph-native analytical engine embedding 12 population genetics procedures within a Neo4j graph database where variants, genotypes, genes, and pathways coexist as a single queryable structure. Applied to the complete 3K Rice Genomes dataset (29.6M~SNPs, 3{,}024~accessions), GraphPop's annotation-conditioned queries provide, to our knowledge, the first genome-wide quantification of relaxed purifying selection across all 12 Asian rice subpopulations ($\pi_N/\pi_S > 1.0$ in every subpopulation), and the first direct detection of adaptive protein divergence at the temperate--tropical japonica boundary (missense Fst/synonymous Fst~$= 1.11$)---analyses that are inaccessible to existing tools. Graph-persistent results enable multi-statistic convergence analysis, recovering known domestication genes (GW5, Hd1, PROG1, Wx) where XP-EHH, PBS, and Fay \& Wu's $H$ signals converge on the same nodes. GraphPop achieves up to 1{,}614$\times$ speedup over scikit-allel at constant 150~MB memory. Pre-built graph databases for rice 3K and 1000 Genomes are publicly available; an MCP server enables AI-agent-driven genomic investigation.
\end{abstract}
